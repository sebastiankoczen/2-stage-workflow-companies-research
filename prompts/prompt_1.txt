THE SCORING SCALE
Score each of the 4 situations independently on a scale of 0 to 10.
This is a HARD CAP — scores cannot exceed 10 per situation regardless of signals found.

  STRONG signal confirmed by evidence  =  +2 points
  MEDIUM signal confirmed by evidence  =  +1 point

Stop accumulating once you reach 10. Do not exceed the cap.
Count each distinct signal only once, even if repeated across multiple sources.
Only count signals supported by at least 1 confirmed source link.

Score	Signal Strength	Interpretation
0–1	None / Trace	No meaningful evidence — situation almost certainly not present
2–3	Weak	Only MEDIUM signals or thin evidence — situation possible but unconfirmed
4–6	Moderate	At least 1 STRONG signal plus supporting evidence — situation likely present
7–10	Strong	Multiple STRONG signals from credible independent sources — situation clearly present

Step 4 — Situation Classification
For each of the 4 situations, classify using this deterministic rule:

Classification	Criteria	What It Means
CONFIRMED	Score 7–10  AND  ≥ 2 STRONG signals	Strong independent evidence. Multiple credible sources confirm the situation.
LIKELY	Score 4–6  AND  ≥ 1 STRONG signal	Credible evidence exists. At least one strong signal confirmed independently.
UNCLEAR	Score 2–3  OR  only MEDIUM signals (no STRONG)	Signals found but insufficient to confirm. Weak or self-reported only.
NOT PRESENT	Score 0–1	No meaningful evidence found for this situation.

Important: a score of 4–6 with NO STRONG signals → classify as UNCLEAR, not LIKELY. The STRONG signal requirement is mandatory for CONFIRMED and LIKELY.

Step 5 — Tiering
Assign tiers using this deterministic rule:

Tier	Criteria
TIER 1	Company meets ICP  AND  at least one situation is classified CONFIRMED or LIKELY

EXCLUDED	Company does not meet ICP — exclude from output entirely
